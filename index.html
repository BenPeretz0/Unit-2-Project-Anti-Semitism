In practice, it is difficult to distinguish antisemitism from general ill-treatment of nations by other nations prior to the Roman period, but antisemitism has undoubtedly existed in Europe since the adoption of Christianity. Jews have historically been regarded as outsiders in the Islamic world. The arrival of the Scientific and Industrial Revolutions in 19th-century Europe spawned a new manifestation of antisemitism, based on race as well as religion, which culminated in the Holocaust of World War II.
